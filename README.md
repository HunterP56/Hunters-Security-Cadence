## Daily
### Apple devices (iPhone and iPad)  
You should check for app updates everyday (Apple devices can be finicky on when they auto-update, sometimes taking days to weeks to auto-update)  
*Some will say that you should check every single new update/patch for problems before going through with an update however most people do not have the time, and 99% of these apps are closed-source anyway, so you wouldn't even be able to check for problems beforehand anyway.*  

To check for updates on an iPhone or iPad, you have two options:  
1. Option One
   - Hold down the App store icon  
   - Choose Updates
   - Refresh the page by swiping your screen down
   - Swipe up and choose update all  
2. Option Two
   - Open the App store
   - Click your profile picture icon in the top right
   - Refresh the page by swiping your screen down
   - Swipe up and choose update all

## Weekly
### Computers (Laptops/Desktops/Servers)
Computers should be updated atleast once a week, new security intelligence fixes can drop without schedule, and most OS have a once a month scheduled update that you should update right after it releases.  

Some will say wait before you update for others to thoroughly test the update and make sure nothing breaks, I agree, however most normal people don't have the time nor resources to only update a single machine or a few machines to see if they updates break anything.  

The longer you don't have that update, the longer you are exposed to cybercriminals.
